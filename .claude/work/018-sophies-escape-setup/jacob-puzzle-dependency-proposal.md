# Puzzle and item dependency proposal: Sophie's Escape: The Witch's Castle

This proposal sets out one puzzle for each of the ten rooms, the items found in each room, the items needed from other rooms, the solution, and the three hints. The aim is a coherent, fair, fun thirty- to sixty-minute play through, scaled from easy in Room 1 to hardest in Room 9, with a satisfying combination puzzle at the gate in Room 10.

Sophie can roam freely between rooms. There are no locked doors. The natural sequence is created by which items unlock which puzzles. Sophie can pick up items in any order, but a puzzle only resolves once she has the items it needs.

The design brief's canonical example is honoured. To open the Library cabinet in Room 4, Sophie needs a key found in the Kitchen in Room 3.

This is a proposal. Anything in it can change.

## Story spine

Sophie wakes in a damp dungeon cell at the bottom of the witch's castle. Her only way out is up and through. The witch watches from her crystal ball in a room Sophie cannot enter. As Sophie climbs through the castle, she collects ingredients, keys, sigils, and a star reading. At the gate, she combines a key from the Armoury, a star reading from the Tower, and a binding charm from the Witch's Study to break the gate's enchantment and step out into daylight.

## Room 1: The Dungeon Cell

The first room. The puzzle teaches Sophie how to look, pick up, and use an item.

### The puzzle

A heavy door blocks Sophie's way out. The door has a rusted lock. Beneath a loose stone in the floor, Sophie can find a bent spoon. The spoon is just strong enough to lift the rusted lock pin.

### Items found in this room

- Bent spoon (under the loose stone).
- Candle stub (on a small shelf).

### Items needed from other rooms

None.

### The solution

Sophie uses the bent spoon on the cell door. The door creaks open. The candle stub goes into her inventory for later.

### Hints

- Hint 1: The cell is not completely empty. Look at the floor as well as the walls.
- Hint 2: One of the floor stones is not seated flush. See what is under it.
- Hint 3: The bent spoon is thin enough to lift the lock pin. Use it on the door.

## Room 2: The Stone Corridor

The hub corridor connects most of the other rooms. The puzzle rewards careful looking.

### The puzzle

A small alcove halfway down the corridor holds a single hidden item: a moonflower petal pressed between two stones. The petal is a Kitchen ingredient.

A second item, an oil-soaked rag, is folded into a sconce.

### Items found in this room

- Moonflower petal (hidden in a wall alcove).
- Oil-soaked rag (folded into a wall sconce).

### Items needed from other rooms

None.

### The solution

Sophie picks up both items. The petal feeds the Kitchen puzzle. The rag combines with the candle stub from Room 1 to make a lit torch, which is used in the Great Hall in Room 5.

### Hints

- Hint 1: The corridor is long. Walk its full length and look at every wall.
- Hint 2: Light fixtures and gaps between stones can hide small items.
- Hint 3: The alcove halfway down holds a petal. The sconce above it holds a rag.

## Room 3: The Kitchen

A working witch's kitchen with a bubbling cauldron, ingredient shelves, and a recipe pinned to the wall.

### The puzzle

A recipe pinned above the cauldron lists three ingredients for a small iron key: salt, dried mushroom, and moonflower petal. Salt and dried mushroom sit on the shelves. The moonflower petal is hidden in the Stone Corridor. When all three drop into the cauldron, the brew hardens in a key-shaped mould beside the hearth.

### Items found in this room

- Pinch of salt (shelf).
- Dried mushroom (shelf).
- Key-shaped iron mould (beside the cauldron, fixed in place).

### Items needed from other rooms

- Moonflower petal from the Stone Corridor (Room 2).

### The solution

Sophie drops all three ingredients into the cauldron. The cauldron hisses, the brew sets in the mould, and a small iron key appears. This is the Library cabinet key.

### Hints

- Hint 1: The witch has left her recipe out. Read it carefully.
- Hint 2: Two of the three ingredients are on the shelves. The third is not in this room.
- Hint 3: The moonflower petal is in the Stone Corridor. Drop all three into the cauldron.

## Room 4: The Library

Tall bookshelves, a locked cabinet, a reading desk with scrolls.

### The puzzle

A locked cabinet holds a torn page from a spell book. The cabinet opens with the small iron key from the Kitchen. On the desk, a scroll lists six symbols in a specific order. This scroll is the clue Sophie needs in the Chapel.

### Items found in this room

- Torn spell book page (inside the cabinet).
- Symbol order scroll (on the reading desk).

### Items needed from other rooms

- Small iron key from the Kitchen (Room 3).

### The solution

Sophie uses the iron key on the cabinet. The cabinet opens. The torn spell book page goes into her inventory. The symbol order scroll, which she can read at any time, gives her the symbol sequence for the Chapel altar.

### Hints

- Hint 1: Two items in this room matter. One is in plain view. One is locked away.
- Hint 2: A key from another room opens the cabinet. The kitchen brews keys.
- Hint 3: Use the iron key on the cabinet. Read the scroll on the desk to learn the symbol order.

## Room 5: The Great Hall

The hub room. A long stone hall with a row of family portraits and a cold fireplace.

### The puzzle

Four portraits hang along the wall. Three of them show the witch's ancestors holding an object. The fourth, the largest, shows an empty hand. The empty hand is a hinge: when Sophie looks at it with a lit torch, a glint reveals that the painting swings out, exposing a hollow behind it. Inside the hollow is the iron key for the Armoury chest.

To find the hinge in the gloom, Sophie needs the lit torch made from her candle stub and the oil-soaked rag.

### Items found in this room

- Armoury chest key (behind the largest portrait).
- A pattern of three objects shown by the other portraits (a chalice, a quill, a star). This pattern is a clue Sophie can read at any time, used in the Witch's Study.

### Items needed from other rooms

- Candle stub from the Dungeon Cell (Room 1).
- Oil-soaked rag from the Stone Corridor (Room 2).

### The solution

Sophie combines the candle stub and the rag in her inventory to make a lit torch. She holds the torch up to the largest portrait. The portrait swings out. She takes the Armoury chest key from the hollow. The chalice, quill, and star pattern is noted in her inventory as a clue for later.

### Hints

- Hint 1: The hall is shadowy. Some details only show in better light.
- Hint 2: A candle alone is too weak. A candle wrapped in cloth burns brighter.
- Hint 3: Combine the candle stub and the oil rag. Hold the torch to the largest portrait.

## Room 6: The Chapel

A small chapel with three stained glass windows and a stone altar. Pattern observation and replication.

### The puzzle

The altar has six round indentations across its face, each with a small disc that Sophie can rotate. Each disc shows one of six symbols. Sophie must rotate them into the order shown on the symbol order scroll from the Library. When the order is correct, a hidden drawer in the altar slides open, revealing a chapel sigil pressed into wax.

### Items found in this room

- Chapel sigil (inside the altar drawer, once the symbols are set correctly).

### Items needed from other rooms

- Symbol order scroll from the Library (Room 4). This is observed, not consumed.

### The solution

Sophie reads the scroll, rotates the six altar discs to match the order, and the drawer opens. The chapel sigil goes into her inventory. It is needed in the Witch's Study.

### Hints

- Hint 1: The altar wants an order, not a single answer. Where might you find an order written down?
- Hint 2: The Library scroll lists six symbols in a specific order. Use that order on the altar.
- Hint 3: Set the six altar discs in the exact sequence the scroll shows, left to right.

## Room 7: The Armoury

Decorative weapons on the walls, a heavy wooden chest in the centre, a rack of helmets.

### The puzzle

A heavy wooden chest sits in the middle of the floor. The chest is locked. The key is the one Sophie found behind the largest portrait in the Great Hall.

### Items found in this room

- Iron gate key (inside the chest).

### Items needed from other rooms

- Armoury chest key from the Great Hall (Room 5).

### The solution

Sophie uses the chest key on the chest. The lid lifts with a creak. Inside, on a velvet cushion, lies a large iron gate key. This is one of the three items needed for the Castle Gate.

### Hints

- Hint 1: The chest holds something important. The chest needs a key.
- Hint 2: The chest key is not in the Armoury. Look behind a painting in the Great Hall.
- Hint 3: Use the Armoury chest key on the chest. Take the iron gate key.

## Room 8: The Tower Room

At the top of a winding stair. A telescope on a tripod, a star map painted on the wall, and a wide window onto the night sky.

### The puzzle

The telescope can be turned left or right and tilted up or down. Through the eyepiece, Sophie sees a sky of stars. She must line up the telescope with three specific stars to read a three-symbol code. The star map on the wall shows which three stars to find: the chalice, the quill, and the star, the same three symbols she saw on the Great Hall portraits.

When the telescope is aligned, a small panel on the tripod clicks open and reveals a brass star chart with the three-symbol code etched into it.

### Items found in this room

- Brass star chart (revealed when the telescope is aligned correctly).

### Items needed from other rooms

- The chalice, quill, and star clue noted from the Great Hall portraits (Room 5). Observed, not consumed.

### The solution

Sophie reads the star map on the wall, matches it to the portrait clue from the Great Hall, and aligns the telescope on the three named stars. The panel opens. She takes the brass star chart. It is one of the three items needed for the Castle Gate, and it is also consulted in the Witch's Study.

### Hints

- Hint 1: The telescope is not for show. It can be aimed. The wall map tells you where.
- Hint 2: Three of the stars on the wall map have names. You have seen those three names before, in a painting.
- Hint 3: Aim the telescope at the chalice, the quill, and the star. Open the panel on the tripod.

## Room 9: The Witch's Study

The most atmospheric room. Spell books on every surface. A cold crystal ball on a stand. A reading lectern open at a spell.

### The puzzle

The lectern shows the witch's binding spell, but the page is incomplete. The torn spell book page from the Library completes the spell. The spell calls for three components: a chapel sigil, a star reading, and a charged crystal.

Sophie places the torn page on the lectern. She places the chapel sigil from the Chapel and reads the star reading from the brass star chart, which she sets on a small plate beside the lectern. The crystal ball, still inactive, sits ready. When all three components are in place and Sophie speaks the spell (a single click of the cast button), the crystal ball lights up briefly and a small charged crystal rises out of it. This is the binding charm.

### Items found in this room

- Charged binding crystal (the binding charm, produced when the spell is cast).

### Items needed from other rooms

- Torn spell book page from the Library (Room 4).
- Chapel sigil from the Chapel (Room 6).
- Brass star chart from the Tower Room (Room 8).

### The solution

With all three items placed and the spell cast, the binding crystal appears. It is the third and last item Sophie needs at the Castle Gate.

### Hints

- Hint 1: The lectern shows a spell, but the page is torn. The missing piece is in another room.
- Hint 2: The spell needs three things from three different rooms. The Library has the page, the Chapel has a sigil, the Tower has a chart.
- Hint 3: Place the torn page, the chapel sigil, and the star chart. Cast the spell. Take the binding crystal.

## Room 10: The Castle Gate

The final room. Heavy iron gates, a stone keystone above them, and a small pedestal in front.

### The puzzle

Three sockets are set into the pedestal:

- A keyhole, for the iron gate key from the Armoury.
- A round indentation, for the brass star chart from the Tower Room.
- A small cradle in the centre, for the charged binding crystal from the Witch's Study.

The order does not matter. When all three items are in place, the keystone glows, the witch's binding on the gate snaps, and the iron gates swing open onto daylight.

### Items found in this room

None. The Castle Gate is the destination, not a source of items.

### Items needed from other rooms

- Iron gate key from the Armoury (Room 7).
- Brass star chart from the Tower Room (Room 8).
- Charged binding crystal from the Witch's Study (Room 9).

### The solution

Sophie places all three items in their sockets. The gate opens. The escape cutscene plays. Sophie steps out into daylight.

### Hints

- Hint 1: The pedestal has three sockets. Each one wants something different.
- Hint 2: The hardest rooms each gave you something for this gate. Bring them all here.
- Hint 3: Place the iron key, the brass star chart, and the binding crystal in the pedestal sockets.

## Dependency graph summary

This section lists the full flow of items and clues across the ten rooms. It shows what each room produces, what each room needs from elsewhere, and which rooms feed which.

### What each room produces

- Room 1, Dungeon Cell: bent spoon (used in Room 1), candle stub (used in Room 5).
- Room 2, Stone Corridor: moonflower petal (used in Room 3), oil-soaked rag (used in Room 5).
- Room 3, Kitchen: small iron key (used in Room 4).
- Room 4, Library: torn spell book page (used in Room 9), symbol order scroll (used as a clue in Room 6).
- Room 5, Great Hall: Armoury chest key (used in Room 7), three-symbol portrait clue (used as a clue in Room 8).
- Room 6, Chapel: chapel sigil (used in Room 9).
- Room 7, Armoury: iron gate key (used in Room 10).
- Room 8, Tower Room: brass star chart (used in Room 9 and Room 10).
- Room 9, Witch's Study: charged binding crystal (used in Room 10).
- Room 10, Castle Gate: nothing. The gate is the destination.

### What each room needs from elsewhere

- Room 1, Dungeon Cell: nothing.
- Room 2, Stone Corridor: nothing.
- Room 3, Kitchen: moonflower petal from Room 2.
- Room 4, Library: small iron key from Room 3.
- Room 5, Great Hall: candle stub from Room 1, oil-soaked rag from Room 2.
- Room 6, Chapel: symbol order scroll from Room 4 (read, not consumed).
- Room 7, Armoury: Armoury chest key from Room 5.
- Room 8, Tower Room: three-symbol portrait clue from Room 5 (observed, not consumed).
- Room 9, Witch's Study: torn spell book page from Room 4, chapel sigil from Room 6, brass star chart from Room 8.
- Room 10, Castle Gate: iron gate key from Room 7, brass star chart from Room 8, charged binding crystal from Room 9.

### The two pattern rooms

The design brief asks for at least two rooms that use the observe-in-one-room, replicate-in-another mechanic. This proposal includes:

- Chapel and Library. The Library scroll shows a six-symbol order. Sophie replicates that order on the Chapel altar.
- Tower Room and Great Hall. The Great Hall portraits show three named symbols (chalice, quill, star). Sophie finds the same three names on the Tower star map and aligns the telescope on them.

### How the difficulty scales

- Rooms 1 and 2 are warm-up: look, pick up, use. No combinations.
- Room 3 is a first combination puzzle inside a single room. One ingredient comes from elsewhere.
- Rooms 4, 5, and 6 each use one or two cross-room dependencies.
- Room 7 is short but gates the final item flow.
- Room 8 adds an observational interaction (aiming the telescope).
- Room 9 is the most complex puzzle, with three items from three different rooms and a spell to cast.
- Room 10 is the satisfying combination of items from the three most complex rooms (7, 8, and 9), which matches the design brief's request for a finale built from the hardest rooms.

### The longest dependency chain

The longest chain runs through nine rooms:

Room 1 candle stub plus Room 2 oil rag, combined to a torch, used in Room 5 to find the Armoury chest key, used in Room 7 to open the chest, producing the iron gate key, used in Room 10. In parallel, Room 4 scroll and Room 6 sigil and Room 8 star chart all feed Room 9, which produces the binding crystal also used in Room 10. The two threads meet at the gate.

### Estimated play time

If each room takes between three and seven minutes for a player who is thinking carefully and using one or two hints, the total run is between thirty and sixty minutes. That sits inside the design brief's estimate.

## Notes for Tim and Sophie

This is a starting point. Anything in it can be renamed, replaced, or rebuilt. If Sophie wants the Kitchen to brew something more dramatic than a key (a sleeping potion, a glow drink, something fun), or the binding crystal to be something more storybook (a star caught in a jar), the dependency graph still works with the names swapped. The structure is what matters: each room produces something, each room consumes something or observes a clue, and the gate at the end takes the three things from the three hardest rooms.

If you want to make the game shorter, the most natural cut is Room 5's torch step, which could be replaced by the painting simply swinging out when Sophie touches the frame. If you want to make it longer, the most natural extension is to add a second ingredient hunt to the Kitchen and a second symbol layer to the Chapel.
